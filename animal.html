Animals

These are some pictures of my favorite animals, but I love all animals so much! I think that animals are very adorable and cute. Which is why it's hard for me to choose which ones I like best, but if I had to choose some, these would be the ones I'd pick:

Polar Bear -

Do You Know? Q1 : How much blubber is under a polar bear’s skin?

Fun Facts About Polar Bears : Polar bears have black skin that appears white, it’s just transparent. It’s also the largest carnivore that lives on land. Polar bears use sea ice as a platform to hunt seals, which makes up most of its diet, and they can sense them from a mile away. Polar bears have 42 teeth, and their scientific name is ‘ursus maritimus’.

Sea Turtle -

Do You Know? Q2 : How old is the oldest known sea turtle fossil?

Fun Facts About Sea Turtles : The Sea Turtles are one of the oldest creatures in the world, and they were also living with dinosaurs, and they can live up to around 100 years. Turtles slow their heart rate to up to nine minutes in between heart beats to conserve oxygen! Only one in every thousand of turtle hatchlings survive all the way to adulthood. A hatchling’s gender depends on the temperature of the nest. Hotter temperatures, females, and cooler temperatures, males. Turtles cry because they have things in their head that help to filter extra salt out of their eyes, which look like they’re crying.

Red Panda -

Do You Know? Q3 : How long can Red Pandas live up to?

Fun Facts About Red Pandas : Red Pandas are Instantly recognisable by their red colouring, long bushy tails, racoon like features, and excellent tree climbers and are able to descend trees head first. Considered endangered, there are fewer than 10,000 in the wild with the largest threats and no habitat. They don’t have a thumb, just an extended wrist bone, which grows over time to help them climb trees and eat bamboo. They’re vegetarian carnivores, they rarely eat anything more than bamboo and a few insects. The red panda diet is 97% bamboo and they occasionally eat small mammals, eggs, and flowers. The sound they make is called “twittering”. Red pandas live in the Eastern Himalayas and South-western China, they are the size of a large domestic cat.
